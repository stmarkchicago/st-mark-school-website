*Editor's Note: This guest post is from Katherine Williams, mother of two St. Mark students. We asked her to share why she chose Montessori education for her children.*

---

Three years ago, my husband and I began searching for the right school for our daughter. We knew we wanted a Christian environment, but we also wanted something more—an education that would cultivate her natural curiosity and love for learning.

That's when a friend told us about St. Mark Christian School and their Montessori approach.

## Discovering Montessori

Honestly, I didn't know much about Montessori education. I had vague impressions—something about hands-on learning and kids working at their own pace. But as I toured St. Mark and observed the classrooms, I was captivated.

### What I Saw
- Children deeply engaged in purposeful work
- A calm, ordered environment (not chaotic like I expected)
- Teachers guiding rather than lecturing
- Students helping one another naturally
- Joy in learning—not because of rewards, but for its own sake

## Why Montessori Fits Our Faith

What surprised me most was how beautifully Montessori philosophy aligns with our Christian values:

### 1. Respect for the Child
Montessori sees each child as a unique creation of God, designed with inherent dignity and purpose. The teacher's role is to help unveil the child God created them to be—not to force them into a predetermined mold.

This resonated deeply with me. God made my daughter with specific gifts, interests, and a unique timeline for development. Montessori education honors that.

### 2. Cultivating Virtue
Montessori classrooms naturally teach:
- **Self-discipline** - Children learn to manage their own time and choices
- **Order** - Everything has its place, reflecting God's orderly creation
- **Service** - Older students help younger ones; we care for our environment
- **Patience** - Deep work takes time; we don't rush learning
- **Gratitude** - We appreciate beautiful materials and meaningful work

### 3. Wonder and Worship
Maria Montessori spoke of children having a "cosmic education"—understanding their place in God's grand design. At St. Mark, this cosmic perspective is explicitly Christian.

My children learn that:
- Math reveals God's precise design
- Language is a gift that allows us to communicate truth
- Science uncovers the wonders of creation
- History shows God's faithfulness through the ages
- Art allows us to create as image-bearers of the Creator

## The Results We've Seen

Our daughter started at St. Mark three years ago, and our son joined last year. The changes we've witnessed are remarkable:

### Academic Growth
- Reading well above grade level
- Tackling complex mathematical concepts independently
- Engaging with challenging literature
- Asking deep, thoughtful questions

### Character Development
- Increased independence and confidence
- Natural compassion for others
- Ability to focus for extended periods
- Intrinsic motivation (not dependent on external rewards)
- Problem-solving mindset

### Spiritual Formation
- Praying spontaneously throughout the day
- Seeing God in everyday moments
- Asking theological questions
- Serving others joyfully
- Memorizing Scripture naturally

## Not Just About Academics

What I appreciate most is that St. Mark doesn't just fill my children's minds—they're forming their hearts. The teachers genuinely know my kids, pray for them, and partner with us in discipleship.

When my daughter struggled with a friendship issue, her teacher didn't just mediate—she helped both girls think through biblical principles like forgiveness and reconciliation. That's the kind of formation you can't get in most schools.

## Is Montessori for Everyone?

Some parents worry that Montessori is too permissive or that children "just play all day." That's not what I've seen. Yes, children have freedom—but it's freedom within limits, purpose, and order.

The environment is carefully prepared. The materials are intentional. The work is meaningful. And throughout it all, Christ is central.

Is Montessori right for every child? Maybe not. But for families who want:
- An approach that honors how God designed children to learn
- Academic rigor with heart formation
- Independent, joyful learners
- A community that shares your faith and values

...St. Mark's Montessori program is a treasure.

## Come See for Yourself

If you're curious about Montessori education, I encourage you to tour St. Mark. Seeing it in action was what convinced me.

Watch children work with the materials. Observe the peace and focus in the classrooms. Talk to the teachers. You'll see what I mean.

Our family is so grateful we discovered St. Mark. It's been a blessing beyond what we imagined.
