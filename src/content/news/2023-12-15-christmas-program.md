What a beautiful evening we shared as our St. Mark community gathered to celebrate the birth of our Savior!

## The True Meaning of Christmas

Our annual Christmas program, held on December 14th, brought together families, friends, and community members for an evening of worship and wonder. Over 100 students participated, sharing the gospel story through:

- Traditional carols and hymns
- Scripture readings from the Nativity accounts
- Drama presentations
- Instrumental performances
- Candlelight procession

## Student Reflections

The program opened with our youngest students singing "Away in a Manger," their sweet voices reminding us of the humble circumstances of Christ's birth. Elementary students presented dramatic readings from Luke 2 and Matthew 1-2, helping us reflect on the magnitude of God becoming man.

### Special Highlights
- Fifth-grade string ensemble's beautiful rendition of "O Come, O Come Emmanuel"
- Kindergarten students' living nativity scene
- Sixth-grade choir's four-part harmony on "Silent Night"
- Father Michael's meditation on the Incarnation

## A Heart of Gratitude

Thank you to our dedicated music and drama teachers, parent volunteers, and all who helped make this evening possible. Most of all, we give thanks to God for the gift of His Son.

As Mrs. Roberts, our music director, said: "This program isn't just a performance—it's our worship offering. We want to point everyone to Jesus, the reason for our celebration."

## Looking Ahead

Our next community gathering will be the Epiphany Chapel on January 6th at 10:00 AM. We hope to see you there as we continue celebrating the revelation of Christ to the nations.
