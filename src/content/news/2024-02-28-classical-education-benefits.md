In an age of constant change and information overload, parents often ask: "What kind of education will truly prepare my child for the future?" At St. Mark, we believe the answer lies not in chasing trends, but in time-tested classical education united with Christian formation.

## What Is Classical Education?

Classical education follows the developmental stages of learning known as the Trivium:

### The Grammar Stage (Early Childhood - 4th Grade)
Young children naturally love to memorize and absorb information. In these years, we fill their minds with:
- Rich language and vocabulary
- Poetry, hymns, and Scripture
- Historical facts and timelines
- Mathematical operations
- The beauty of God's creation

### The Logic Stage (5th - 8th Grade)
As children's analytical abilities develop, we teach them to think critically:
- Logical reasoning and argumentation
- Connecting ideas across disciplines
- Asking "why" questions
- Formal logic training
- Understanding cause and effect in history and science

### The Rhetoric Stage (9th - 12th Grade)
Mature students learn to express truth persuasively:
- Written and oral communication
- Defending ideas gracefully
- Creating original work
- Applying knowledge to complex problems
- Speaking truth with love

## Why Classical + Montessori?

At St. Mark, we uniquely blend classical education with Montessori methods. While classical education provides the content and progression, Montessori offers the means:
- Hands-on learning materials
- Self-directed work
- Mixed-age communities
- Following the child's natural development
- Teacher as guide rather than lecturer

## The Christian Difference

What makes our classical education distinctly Christian? We believe:
- **All truth is God's truth** - Every subject reveals something about our Creator
- **Education shapes the soul** - We're not just filling minds but forming hearts
- **Virtue matters** - We cultivate wisdom, courage, temperance, and justice
- **The great books point to the Greatest Book** - Literature, history, and philosophy find their fulfillment in Scripture

## Preparing for an Unknown Future

Some ask if classical education is outdated. We respond: The world changes, but human nature doesn't. Students who can think critically, communicate clearly, love truth, and pursue virtue will thrive in any era.

Our graduates don't just know facts—they know how to learn, think, and grow throughout their lives. That's an education that lasts.
